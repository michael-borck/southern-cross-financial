# Privacy and Client Data Protection Policy

**Document Version:** 3.2  
**Last Updated:** 15 March 2024  
**Next Review Date:** 15 March 2025  
**Approved By:** Greg Morrison, Managing Director  
**Policy Owner:** Annabel Tan, Head of Compliance & Risk  
**Document Classification:** Confidential - Internal Use Only

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This Privacy and Client Data Protection Policy outlines Southern Cross Financial's commitment to protecting the privacy and confidentiality of client personal information in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth), Australian Financial Services Licence obligations under the Corporations Act 2001, and ASIC regulatory requirements.

This policy applies to all Southern Cross Financial employees, contractors, advisers, and third-party service providers who have access to client personal information in the course of providing financial services.

**Effective Date:** This policy supersedes all previous versions and takes effect immediately upon approval.

## 2. Regulatory Framework

Southern Cross Financial operates under AFSL 234567 and must comply with:

- Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and Australian Privacy Principles (APPs)
- Corporations Act 2001, particularly sections relating to client confidentiality
- ASIC Regulatory Guides, including RG 271 (Internal dispute resolution)
- Notifiable Data Breaches scheme under Part IIIC of the Privacy Act
- Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorism Financing Act 2006

*Note: Following the Banking Royal Commission recommendations, enhanced record-keeping obligations came into effect January 2023. Compliance team continues to monitor proposed changes to financial advice documentation requirements.*

## 3. Collection of Personal Information

### 3.1 Types of Information Collected

Southern Cross Financial collects personal information necessary to provide comprehensive financial services, including:

**Identity and Contact Information:**
- Full name, date of birth, residential address
- Email addresses, phone numbers
- Identification documents (driver's licence, passport)
- Tax File Numbers (where legally required)

**Financial Information:**
- Income details, employment history
- Assets, liabilities, and investment portfolios
- Banking details and transaction histories
- Insurance policies and beneficiary information
- Superannuation fund details

**Personal Circumstances:**
- Family situation and dependents
- Health information (for insurance advice only)
- Risk tolerance and investment objectives
- Estate planning preferences

### 3.2 Collection Methods

Personal information is collected through:
- Initial client discovery meetings with financial planners
- Client onboarding forms (digital and paper-based)
- Ongoing review meetings and annual check-ins
- Third-party sources (with client consent) including fund managers, insurance providers
- Public registers and databases where legally permitted

*Implementation Note: Digital onboarding platform launched Q4 2023 through partnership with AdviserLogic. Paper forms remain available for clients preferring traditional processes.*

### 3.3 Collection Notices

All clients must receive a Privacy Collection Notice before or at the time of information collection, explaining:
- What information we collect and why
- How information will be used and disclosed
- Storage and security arrangements
- Client rights regarding their personal information
- Contact details for privacy inquiries

**Responsibility:** Naomi Street's financial planning team ensures collection notices are provided during initial client meetings. Marketing team (Lisa Kwan) maintains current notices on website and client portal.

## 4. Use of Personal Information

### 4.1 Primary Purposes

Southern Cross Financial uses client personal information to:

- Provide comprehensive financial planning and wealth management services
- Prepare Statements of Advice (SOAs) and ongoing strategy reviews
- Facilitate investment transactions and portfolio management
- Process insurance applications and claims
- Conduct compliance monitoring and risk management
- Maintain client relationship management systems

### 4.2 Secondary Purposes

With appropriate client consent or where legally permitted:
- Direct marketing of additional financial services
- Client satisfaction surveys and service improvement initiatives
- Aggregated reporting for business planning (de-identified data only)
- Compliance with regulatory reporting obligations

### 4.3 Internal Access Controls

**Client Relationship Teams:** Full access to assigned client files for service delivery
**Compliance Team:** Access for audit, monitoring, and regulatory reporting purposes
**Finance Team:** Limited access for billing, fee calculation, and payment processing
**IT Support:** System access only, with activity logging and approval protocols

*Current access management through Active Directory, scheduled for upgrade to role-based permissions platform by June 2024.*

## 5. Disclosure of Personal Information

### 5.1 Authorised Disclosures

Southern Cross Financial may disclose personal information to:

**Service Partners:**
- Platform providers (Netwealth, HUB24) for investment administration
- Insurance providers for application processing and claims
- Custodial services for portfolio management
- External compliance consultants (currently engaged: Compliance Partners WA)

**Professional Advisers:**
- Accountants for tax planning coordination
- Legal practitioners for estate planning
- Mortgage brokers for lending referrals
- Specialist advisers (SMSFs, property, etc.)

**Regulatory Bodies:**
- ASIC for licence compliance and investigation purposes
- AUSTRAC for AML/CTF reporting obligations
- Australian Taxation Office where legally required
- Financial Ombudsman Service for dispute resolution

### 5.2 Third-Party Agreements

All service providers with access to client personal information must:
- Sign comprehensive confidentiality agreements
- Maintain equivalent privacy protections
- Submit annual compliance declarations
- Undergo security assessments (major providers only)

**Current major provider reviews:** Netwealth (completed Feb 2024), HUB24 (due April 2024), AdviserLogic (pending initial assessment).

### 5.3 Overseas Disclosures

Limited overseas disclosures may occur for:
- International investment platforms (US-based custodians)
- Cloud storage services with Australian data sovereignty requirements
- Global insurance providers for high-net-worth clients

**Notice Requirement:** Clients are informed of potential overseas disclosures in collection notices and service agreements.

## 6. Data Storage and Security

### 6.1 Physical Security

**Perth Office (Level 8, 108 St Georges Terrace):**
- Swipe card access to premises (Level 8 only)
- Locked filing cabinets for confidential documents
- Clean desk policy enforced after hours
- Visitor access log and escort requirements
- CCTV monitoring (retention: 30 days)

### 6.2 Digital Security

**Infrastructure:**
- Microsoft 365 Business Premium with Advanced Threat Protection
- Multi-factor authentication for all financial services applications
- Encrypted email for confidential communications (MailGuard Plus)
- Regular security updates and patch management
- Offsite backup with 7-year retention policy

**Access Controls:**
- Individual user accounts with complex password requirements
- Role-based permissions aligned with job responsibilities
- Quarterly access reviews conducted by IT and Compliance teams
- Remote access through VPN only (Cisco AnyConnect)

### 6.3 Document Retention

**Financial Planning Records:** 7 years from advice delivery (ASIC requirement)
**AML/CTF Records:** 7 years from transaction completion
**General Client Records:** 7 years from relationship termination
**Marketing Preferences:** Until client withdrawal or 5 years of inactivity

*Note: Recent ASIC enforcement actions have emphasised robust record retention. Compliance team conducting file audit through Q2 2024 to ensure compliance.*

## 7. Client Rights and Access

### 7.1 Access Requests

Clients may request access to their personal information by:
- Contacting their financial adviser directly
- Emailing privacy@southerncross.com.au
- Writing to Privacy Officer, Southern Cross Financial

**Response Timeframes:**
- Acknowledgment within 2 business days
- Response within 30 calendar days
- Complex requests may require extension (client notification required)

### 7.2 Correction Requests

Clients may request correction of personal information where:
- Information is inaccurate, out-of-date, incomplete, irrelevant, or misleading
- Corrections affect financial advice or product recommendations
- Updates are required for regulatory compliance

**Correction Process:** Annabel Tan (Compliance) coordinates with relevant adviser to verify and implement corrections. Client notification within 10 business days.

## 8. Data Breach Response

### 8.1 Incident Classification

**Low Risk:** Inadvertent disclosure to incorrect client (same adviser)
**Medium Risk:** Unauthorised access by staff member or system error
**High Risk:** External cyber attack, theft of devices, or systemic exposure

### 8.2 Response Protocol

1. **Immediate Containment** (within 2 hours)
   - Secure affected systems or documents
   - Notify Annabel Tan (Compliance) and Greg Morrison (MD)
   - Engage external IT security consultants if required

2. **Assessment and Investigation** (within 24 hours)
   - Determine scope and nature of breach
   - Assess likelihood of serious harm to individuals
   - Document all findings and remediation actions

3. **Notification Requirements** (within 72 hours if notifiable)
   - OAIC notification for eligible data breaches
   - Affected client notification if serious harm likely
   - ASIC notification where financial services implicated

### 8.3 Business Continuity

**Data Recovery:** Backup systems maintained through Microsoft 365 and local Perth data centre
**Alternative Communications:** Mobile phones, personal email (encrypted) for critical client communications
**Vendor Support:** 24/7 support agreements with primary technology vendors
